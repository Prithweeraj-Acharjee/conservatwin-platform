My name is Prithweeraj Acharjee. I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Toledo, and I've built an open-source platform called **ConservaTwin** that brings industrial-grade environmental monitoring to museums — at essentially zero cost.

**The problem:** Most museums lack continuous environmental monitoring. Damage from temperature and humidity fluctuations is gradual and invisible until it's too late — cracking paint, foxing on paper, mold on textiles. Commercial monitoring systems cost tens of thousands of dollars.

**The solution:** ConservaTwin uses a $5 WiFi sensor (ESP32 + DHT22) that reads temperature and humidity every 30 seconds. The data flows to our free cloud platform, which:

- Calculates a **Preservation Risk Index (PRI)** — a 0-100 score quantifying environmental stress on artifacts
- Provides **material-specific AI recommendations** — different advice for oil paintings vs. textiles vs. paper
- Detects **anomalies** like HVAC failures, sensor faults, or unusual humidity spikes
- Generates a **public heritage dashboard** showing your museum's live conservation health

The entire platform is open-source and free. The only hardware cost is ~$5 per sensor node.

## Tips:

- Keep it short — busy people skim
- Lead with "free" and "zero cost" — museums have tight budgets
- Offer a specific, low-commitment next step (free 2-week pilot)
- Mention you're local — face-to-face is always better
- Follow up after 5-7 days if no response
