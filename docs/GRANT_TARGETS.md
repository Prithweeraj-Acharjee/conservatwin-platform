# Grant & Program Targets

Apply to these in order of priority. Most require a working demo (you have one).

---

## Immediate (Apply This Month)

### 1. Awesome Foundation — $1,000 Micro-Grant
- **URL:** https://www.awesomefoundation.org/
- **What:** $1,000 no-strings-attached grant for awesome ideas
- **Requirements:** Just a good idea and a short application
- **Why you'll win:** Open-source + heritage preservation + $5 sensor = perfect fit
- **Deadline:** Rolling (monthly chapters review)

### 2. UToledo Undergraduate Research — Varies
- **Where:** Talk to your CS department / Honors College
- **What:** Funding + course credit for independent research
- **Action:** Email your advisor or department chair THIS WEEK

### 3. GitHub Accelerator — $20,000 + Mentorship
- **URL:** https://accelerator.github.com/
- **What:** $20K stipend, mentorship, GitHub promotion
- **Requirements:** Open-source project with some traction
- **Deadline:** Check for next cohort opening

---

## Short-Term (Within 3 Months)

### 4. Google Summer of Code (GSoC) — $1,500-$6,600
- **URL:** https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/
- **What:** Paid summer internship working on open-source
- **Requirements:** Be a student, have an open-source project
- **Note:** You'd need to register your project as an org or join an existing one

### 5. Mozilla Builders / MOSS — $10,000-$250,000
- **URL:** https://foundation.mozilla.org/en/what-we-fund/awards/
- **What:** Funding for open-source projects with social impact
- **Requirements:** Working project, clear impact narrative

### 6. National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) — Digital Humanities
- **URL:** https://www.neh.gov/grants
- **What:** $10K-$100K for digital humanities projects
- **Requirements:** Academic affiliation (UToledo counts)
- **Note:** Partner with a humanities professor for stronger application

---

## Medium-Term (3-6 Months)

### 7. Getty Foundation — Conservation Grants
- **URL:** https://www.getty.edu/foundation/
- **What:** Major grants for art conservation
- **Requirements:** Usually institutional, but student initiatives considered
- **Strategy:** Partner with Toledo Museum of Art for a joint application

### 8. Knight Foundation — Arts & Technology
- **URL:** https://knightfoundation.org/
- **What:** Funding for technology + arts intersection
- **Requirements:** Clear community impact

### 9. ICCROM Fellowships
- **URL:** https://www.iccrom.org/
- **What:** Conservation research fellowships (UNESCO-affiliated)
- **Requirements:** Working on conservation technology
- **Why:** Direct pipeline to UNESCO network

### 10. Google Arts & Culture
- **URL:** https://artsandculture.google.com/
- **What:** Technology partnerships for cultural heritage
- **Strategy:** Get featured, not funded — visibility leads to everything else

## Competitions

### 11. Hacker News "Show HN"
- **What:** Post your project, get visibility from tech community
- **Strategy:** Post on a Tuesday/Wednesday morning (US time)
- **Title:** "Show HN: Open-source digital twin for museum conservation ($5 sensors)"

### 12. Product Hunt Launch
- **What:** Product launch platform
- **Strategy:** Get a "hunter" to feature you, launch on a weekday

### 13. James Dyson Award — Engineering for Good
- **URL:** https://www.jamesdysonaward.org/
- **What:** £30,000 for engineering students solving problems
- **Requirements:** Student, engineering solution to a real problem

## Strategy Notes

1. **Apply to Awesome Foundation FIRST** — easiest, fastest, builds momentum
2. **Talk to UToledo THIS WEEK** — academic support unlocks everything else
3. **Get one real museum using it** — this is the #1 thing that makes every application stronger
4. **Post on Hacker News** — if it gets traction, grants come to YOU
5. **Build in public** — tweet your progress, post on LinkedIn, write blog posts
